Incontinence is one of the most challenging and sensitive aspects of dementia care. It can feel overwhelming for families and caregivers, yet with compassion and practical strategies, dignity and comfort can be preserved for those living with dementia.

*Did you know?* An estimated **60–70% of people living with dementia experience urinary or fecal incontinence** at some stage. This is not just a medical issue, but a deeply personal one that affects self-esteem and relationships.
